# Barb's Book — character sheet spec (from adversarial playtest synthesis)

BARB'S BOOK — the character sheet, fully diegetic. It is not a menu; it is the tab book Barb keeps by the till, and consulting it is asking to see what she's written about you.

FICTION & FRAME. One lit ledger page over dimmed prose. Everything on it is in Barb's hand, in her voice, in the prose register. It doubles as the NG+ artifact: the same content that persists as the plain-text register file beside the saves (decisions.md, NG+ full diegetic), so the overlay and the file never disagree.

UNLOCK MOMENT. End of the Counter Interview, Night 1. Barb closes the book and says one authored line: 'Ask, if you ever want to know what I've written.' That line IS the tutorial — she teaches it exactly once, in the same cadence as her one warning. CLI: the dim hint ('l consults the ledger') prints once under the next choice list, never again. Web: a small 'consult Barb's book' text button appears bottom-left in title-hint styling and pulses once on unlock.

INTERACTION. CLI: 'l' at any prompt (existing). Web: the button, plus 'l' key for parity. Opens instantly; Esc / click-outside closes with a one-line exit beat ('the book goes back under the counter'). Free action — no time cost, no slot spent, music keeps playing. Redraw must not re-emit scene tell lines (fix-03).

CONTENTS, top to bottom, zero numerals anywhere:
1. THE REGISTER LINE, rendered as a @doc document block — the actual entry: 'Nov 6 — [name column blank] — 1 — supper, tab.' Present from Night 1. The blank NAME column is the sheet's quietest question and it grows heavier as NAME events accrue (entries surviving the night, per the bible's register fiction).
2. HER FIVE OBSERVATIONS — one line per stat, ported from apps/cli/src/ledger.ts into packages/story so CLI and web share one source of truth. Tiers RESCALED to Act-1 range (low <4, mid 4–7, high ≥8, or a fourth tier per stat) so at least two entries visibly change between Day 2 and Day 7 on a warm route — the Pleaser's flatlined instrument is a bug in thresholds, not in concept. The UNDERTOW line must survive every rewrite ('looks at the water the way the water looks at everyone') — it is the only mirror the Refuser's path has.
3. THE STATIC LINE — rewritten tiers: low tier weather-deniable in Barb's voice ('the damp hasn't got into her yet. it gets into everything here.' — the current 'the fog has not found much of her to argue with' names the fog as an agent and violates prose grammar #6); new tier at ≥16 ('the ink fights her lately; I write her twice and keep the second'); existing ≥30/≥60 tiers above that.
4. 'WHAT YOU TOLD HER, NIGHT ONE' — the five interview answers quoted VERBATIM in the player's own words (resolve state.choiceLog for n1-interview-1..5 to their choice labels via content.scenes — '"Two. Heaped."' — never tag slugs). This is the verbatim-player-words-echo graft from decisions.md, given a home.
5. 'MARGINS, OTHER HANDS' — one authored line per FactTag present in state.knownBy.barb (kept-barb-company → 'sat the morning out with me.'; went-to-dianne → 'gave Dianne her first morning.'; refused-first-meal → 'sat a full plate out. wrote it small.'). Entries appear only when Barb witnessed them or gossip reached her — mechanically honest, and it quietly teaches the gossip system by existing.

STATIC DEGRADATION. The page itself is the STATIC meter. Deterministic glyph-rot pass seeded from rngState (same seed → same rot; save-stable): at STATIC ≥16 a few letterforms swap to wrong glyphs; ≥30 random words blur/smear (CSS filter on word spans in web; glyph substitution in CLI); ≥60 contested entries render struck through — 'the letters walk.' Reduced-motion / reduced-flicker mode (a shipped accessibility commitment) renders the same corruption statically, no animation. The CLI gets the identical rot via character substitution — parity is the point: same book, thinner wall.

CLI PARITY. One shared renderer in packages/story emits the page as text blocks; apps/cli prints it in the 72-column frame, apps/web wraps it in the dialog. Nothing exists in one build that doesn't exist in the other.

MUST NEVER SHOW. Bare numbers, stat names (FLESH/NAME/ECHO/UNDERTOW/STATIC/CHORD), meters, bars, deltas, arrows, percentages; flag/tag/fact/choice ids or any internal slug; explanations of why an option was locked; anything about CHORD at all (it surfaces only as music); the fog described as an agent with intent; and never a line Barb could not plausibly have written by hand at the end of a shift.
