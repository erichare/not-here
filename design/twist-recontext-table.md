# Twist Recontext Table

> The twist is LOCKED before any scene is written. Every clue below must be
> planted where stated, must read innocently before the reveal, and must land as
> inevitable after it. When authoring a scene that touches one of these, check
> both columns. Fair-but-surprising is the contract; this table is its ledger.

**The reveal:** you are a fog-construct assembled from five people's curated
memories of Wren, who left seven years ago and is returning on the spring ferry.

## Structural echoes (not clues, but must stay consistent)

- **ECHO's cost** (giver loses the memory) is the twist in mechanical form: you are
  made of their memories; taking more literally transfers her out of them.
- **STATIC glyph-rot** on your own dialogue: the fog reclaiming its material when
  you act against your nature.
- **"Without-you" retellings** (missed scenes) are always slightly wrong in the
  same way the drowning accounts are wrong — Port Lorn's memory is editorial.
  Same narrative device, load-bearing twice.
- **The Wake verdict** turns on FLESH+NAME *witness* — the town literally votes on
  whether you are real, one act before learning the answer.

## Reveal staging

- **Knowing route** (Letter opened, Act 2): the truth arrives as text the player
  reads alone, at night, in Dora's handwriting. Bars 1–5 play complete for the
  first time; the rest where bar 6 should be is held for four seconds; distant horn.
- **Ambush route** (Letter burned): the reveal detonates at the dock in real time —
  the gangway drops mid-ending and recontextualization happens live during the
  finale.
- **Early truth** (high NERVE/UNDERTOW, Dora pressed, Maud's warning heeded): a
  mid-game Act 2 version exists via Maud — she tells you what her husband was. The
  back half becomes a different game (dramatic irony inverted: you know, they
  don't know you know).

## Post-reveal Return Pass

After the reveal (knowing route), revisiting these locations re-renders one line
in each with the second-column reading — never more than one line, never
explained: the shingle (salt), the pub (the ledger), the post office (the circled
date), the lighthouse (Elias's back as he plays). The engine tags these scenes
`recontext:` and the lint requires all four to exist.
