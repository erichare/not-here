# Twist Recontext Table

> The twist is LOCKED before any scene is written. Every clue below must be
> planted where stated, must read innocently before the reveal, and must land as
> inevitable after it. When authoring a scene that touches one of these, check
> both columns. Fair-but-surprising is the contract; this table is its ledger.

## Structural echoes (not clues, but must stay consistent)

- **ECHO's cost** (giver loses the memory) is the twist in mechanical form: you are
  made of their memories; taking more literally transfers her out of them.
- **STATIC glyph-rot** on your own dialogue: the fog reclaiming its material when
  you act against your nature.
- **"Without-you" retellings** (missed scenes) are always slightly wrong in the
  same way the drowning accounts are wrong — Lorn Bay's memory is editorial.
  Same narrative device, load-bearing twice.
- **The photos and the recordings are one clue at two scales:** instruments refuse
  to corroborate you; only people do. Every screen in the game obeys the digital-
  absence doctrine (`retheme-lorn-bay.md`).
- **The potluck verdict** turns on FLESH+NAME *witness* — the town literally votes
  on whether you are real, one act before learning the answer.

## Reveal staging

- **Knowing route** (Letter opened, Act 2): the trail ends at the till drawer of
  Lorn Bay General. The truth arrives as text the player reads alone, at night, in
  Dianne's handwriting. Bars 1–5 play complete for the first time; the rest where
  bar 6 should be is held for four seconds; distant horn.
- **Ambush route** (Letter burned): the reveal detonates at the bus stop in real
  time — the EBUS door hisses open mid-ending and recontextualization happens live
  during the finale, Nov 28.
- **Early truth** (high UNDERTOW, Dianne pressed, Barb's warning heeded): a
  mid-game Act 2 version exists via Barb — she tells you what her husband was. The
  back half becomes a different game (dramatic irony inverted: you know, they
  don't know you know).

## Post-reveal Return Pass

After the reveal (knowing route), revisiting these locations re-renders one line
in each with the second-column reading — never more than one line, never
explained: the beach (no cold in you), the Kettle (the register), the General's
corkboard (the circled card), the wharf (Wade's back as he plays). The engine tags
these scenes `recontext:` and the lint requires all four to exist.
