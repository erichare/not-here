## The keystone

**"Not Here" is the title of Wren's unfinished song.** Five bars, sixth bar a
rest. She wrote it the last winter — a song about being present in a place
that has already decided who you are. The chord sheet in her old room is
headed, in her handwriting: **not here (unfinished)**. She finished it after
she left — after the title finally came true. The horn has been broadcasting
the title over the lake every night at 3:12 for seven years. Nobody in Lorn
Bay says the song's name anymore, which is why nobody notices they live
inside it.

At the title screen, the player thinks the game is named after a mood. At the
reveal, they learn it was named after a song. At their ending, they learn
which meaning was theirs.

## The four readings (distributed across the game)

1. **The song** — what Wren called it (the artifact reading)
2. **You** — what the player character is: a thing that is not, strictly, here
3. **What the town did to Wren** — decided she wasn't here long before she left
4. **The verdict at the end** — whose absence Lorn Bay finally admits

## Escalation ladder (planted → spoken → mechanical → detonated)

### Act 1 — planted, unremarked
- **The register line.** Barb checks you in herself, Night 1. The NAME column
  stays blank. She writes something in it later, off-screen. (Payoff: Act 3 /
  NG+.)
- **The half-heard phone call.** Day 2, Lorn Bay General: Dianne, on the store
  phone, back to you: "No. She's not here. She hasn't been here for a long
  time." Hangs up gently. Carries on. Pre-reveal: a wrong number about
  somebody. Post-reveal: the first true sentence in the game, said eight feet
  from you.
- **The chord sheet.** Wren's room or the wharf shed: `@doc:` artifact —
  a hand-ruled chord chart, five systems filled, the sixth empty, headed
  *not here (unfinished)*. First time the player sees the title inside the
  world. Plays as: a sad song title. Is: the label on their own existence.

### Act 2 — spoken, weight accumulating
- **Barb's one warning** (the only one she gives, per the one-warning rule):
  "My Frank came back, one winter. Sat where you're sitting. He was here —
  and he was not here. And the second one wore the first one out." She never
  explains. She never repeats it.
- **Sam at the memorial potluck:** "That is not my sister. She's not *here*.
  She never came back — whatever this is, it's not her being HERE." The title
  shouted in a community hall, into a casserole silence.
- **Priya's intake form** (`@doc:` artifact): *ID: not provided. DOB: not
  provided. Patient presents as: —* and, in the corner, in doctor's
  shorthand where the player may not even notice it: *pt. not here for exam.*
  Chart language, doing double duty.
- **Wade at the horn, if pressed (NERVE-gated):** "You want to know what I
  play it for. Fine. She stood right there and told me she was already gone —
  'I'm not here, Wade, I haven't been here for years' — and I told her to go
  home to bed. So now the horn says it for her. Every night. Till it's true
  in the other direction."

### Act 3 — mechanical, the interface itself
- **Glyph-rot converges on the title.** At high STATIC, corrupted dialogue
  options degrade toward a fixed attractor: whatever you were going to say
  decays letter by letter into `n o t   h e r e`. The fog reclaims your words
  and signs them with the title. (Visual-parity note: silent players see it
  identically; it's typographic.)
- **The register, seen at last.** When Barb finally shows you your line
  (Act 3, or the Long Winter counsel scene): every previous winter-guest's
  entry has the NAME column filled in her hand with the same two words —
  *not here* — going back forty years to a line that reads only *Frank*.
  Yours is still blank. She has been waiting to see what she'd get to write.
- **The reveal itself** (knowing route): the letter in the till drawer is
  signed off: *"Tell Sam he can stop keeping it. I was never here anyway —
  that was the whole trouble. — W."*

### Endings — each lands on one reading
- **The Sixth Bar:** you finish the song and the last screen names it for the
  only time: *"NOT HERE" — Wren Cole & —* , the co-credit dash where your
  unspoken name goes. The title becomes a finished song and a gift.
- **Two Wrens:** the inversion. She takes your hand on the wharf — first
  unprompted touch in the game — and says: **"You're here."** The only time
  the word is affirmed, from the only person entitled to say it. The title
  denied, as grace.
- **Wren, Again (quiet horror):** the real Wren, at the bus shelter, to a
  town that walked past her: the last line of the game is a stranger's voice
  telling her *"there's nobody here by that name."*
- **Unwitnessed:** Barb's final ledger line, mid-sentence, then the two words
  in her steady hand: *— not here.* The cursor blinks and accepts nothing.
- **The Stranger (4:10 AM bus):** Tam's mileage log gets its second entry in
  seven years: *one passenger, cash. Name given.* The title escaped, once.

## Rules for the thread

1. The exact phrase "not here" may appear **at most once per act** in NPC
   dialogue (the ladder above) — scarcity keeps it load-bearing; the lint
   counts occurrences.
2. Nobody ever says the phrase *about the player* until Sam at the potluck.
3. The song is never called by name in dialogue before the chord sheet is
   seen; after the reveal, characters may finally say "the song's called
   'Not Here'" in confession scenes (each confession = one reading admitted).
4. The title screen adds a subtitle line only after a completed run:
   *"NOT HERE — a song by Wren Cole"* — NG+ players start the game knowing
   the title is diegetic; first-run players never do.
